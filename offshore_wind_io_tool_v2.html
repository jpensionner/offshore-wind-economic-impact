About this model

This tool quantifies the net societal return from public investment in offshore wind across three economic channels: domestic value creation (IO multipliers), power price effects (merit-order), and financing cost (deadweight loss). Results are model outputs, not policy recommendations.

IO multiplier sources — OECD ICIO 2025 edition (all countries)
All five countries — consistent methodology
MethodDomestic Leontief inverse (I−A^d)⁻¹ computed from OECD Inter-Country Input-Output Tables, 2025 edition, reference year 2022. 50 ISIC Rev.4 industries per country. VA and gross output (x) taken directly from explicit VA and OUT rows — no row-sum approximation needed. Sector VA multipliers weighted by gross output share within each of 8 offshore wind supply chain categories.
SourceOECD ICIO 2025 (2022_SML.csv). Domestic intermediate block Z^d for each country. Extraction: scripts/extract_oecd_icio.py. Consistent cross-country methodology — single source for NO, UK, DE, CL, KOR.
Country-specific notes
Norway (0.829)High energy-supply sector share (~28% gross output, driven by oil & gas B06). B06 assigned to Energy supply, not Maritime, to avoid inflating maritime multiplier. Maritime & offshore cluster (B07/B08/B09, C301 shipbuilding, H50/H51): VA mult 0.639.
United Kingdom (0.803)Large services economy; other industries 52% of gross output. Construction VA mult 0.803, reflecting deep domestic supply chain. Energy supply VA mult 0.542 — import-intensive sector.
Germany (0.769)Manufacturing dominates at 27.5% gross output share — highest of all five countries. Electrical & electronics VA mult 0.657. Energy supply VA mult 0.720.
Chile (0.786)Empirical OECD data — no longer estimated. Maritime & offshore VA mult 0.830 — highest of all countries, reflecting large mining support services and port logistics cluster. No offshore wind support scheme yet; policy development case.
South Korea (0.681)Lowest overall VA multiplier — highly export-integrated manufacturing (33.6% gross output). World's largest shipbuilder; Maritime VA mult 0.590. Manufacturing VA mult 0.538, reflecting import-intensive global supply chains.
